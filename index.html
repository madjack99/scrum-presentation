SCRUM

Quick explanation

  • Agile approach
  • Smaller tasks
  • Backlog
  • Sprint (2 - 4 weeks)

Overview

  • Simple concept that can be challenging to successfully implement
  • Helps to tackle larger projects easier
  • Goes beyond the standard management command and control model
  • Relies on an active leadership role (playing coach)
  • Dependent on having knowledgeable, dedicated, and hardworking personnel on a project
  • The people who use scrum the most are the managers and team leaders who are in charge of several groups or departments

History

  • Word Scrum comes from the game of Rugby
  • Scrum goes back to 1986 when Hirotaka Takeuchi and Ikujiro Nonaka first used the term to describe a more efficient way to approach product development
  • 1990’s Ken Schwaber and Jeff Sutherland used and refined this method specifically for software development
  • Has been adopted and modified to work with nearly any project

The Pros and Cons of Scrum

  • Dependent on the nature of the project and who is on your team
  • It’s not a magic button that will suddenly solve all the problems
  • Facilitates efficient ways to utilize time and money

Pros:

  1. Team members have a clearer picture of their tasks.
  2. Feedback from customers and backers can be easily integrated during the process. Big projects are divided up into smaller parts.
  3. Big projects are divided up into smaller parts.
  4. What is being produced can be reviewed and tested.
  5. It’s easy to know how much work and effort individual team members are contributing to the project.

Cons:

Vulnerable to the human element as anything else

  1. Scope Creep.
  2. Team members have to be experienced.
  3. Large teams can have trouble adapting to and fully utilizing the agile nature of the Scrum method.
  4. Quality assurance can be a problem, even with testing.
  5. Losing a team member can have major negative consequences for both the project and their team.

scrum workflow

Sprints

  1. No more than a month long, most commonly two weeks.
  2. Meeting at the start of the sprint -> backlog
  3. Idea -> Code -> Test.
  4. Daily Scrum meeting.
  5. Sprint results demonstration.
  6. Sprint retrospective

scrum workflow

Artifacts

  1. Product itself.
  2. Product backlog prioritized by PO.
  3. Sprint backlog.
  4. Sprint burn-down chart and release burn-down chart.

Scrum specific roles

  • Scrum master
  • Product owner
  • Team itself

Scrum master

  • The team's coach
  • Not a project manager
  • Shelters the team from distractions
  • Helps the team be the best

Product owner

  • Directs the team to the right goal
  • Creates a compelling vision of the product
  • Creates a product backlog
  • Prioritizes the backlog during Scrum development

Team itself

  • Titles are insignificant
  • Individuals are expected to work beyond their preferred disciplines
  • Each person contributes in whatever way they can
